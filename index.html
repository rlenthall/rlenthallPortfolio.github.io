I crafted this dice box as a gift for a friend that I play Dungeons and Dragons with. It is entirely hand made using tools such as a circular saw and power sander. The whole process took just a few days, and it is still holding together after a few years.

For my high school's 2021 production of "Grease", I constructed the "car" that the characters drove around in. It was capable of rolling and supporting the weight of several people. I worked together with another person that did the painting on the side to make it fit the style of the show.

I made this wood frame and string art for a charity auction event at the yacht club that I learned to sail at. It was one of my first woodworking projects, and it only took about a day. It sold for a few hundred dollars, and the winner donated it back to the yach club so that it could be displayed. Working on this project was a foundational moment in my life that helped me realize how much I love combining working with my hands and creating art.

These photos are of different theater projects I worked on in high school. The first photo is an article written about a stage play that I wrote and directed titled "The Tools of a Jester". I put on this play independetly from school or any other organization. The second and third photos are of me in my costumes for "Into the Woods" as the Mysterious Man and "The Crucible" as John Proctor. Through all of my experience in theater, I feel as though I have a very in-depth understanding of what is important both on and off-stage.
